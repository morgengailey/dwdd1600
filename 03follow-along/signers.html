Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

some of the most famous signers include:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, first and most prominent signature on the Declaration of Independence.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence, later became the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned polymath, diplomat, and one of the Founding Fathers who contributed to the drafting of the Declaration.
John Adams
Advocate for independence, later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Leader in the American Revolution and a key figure in organizing resistance against British policies.
Roger Sherman
Only person to sign all four great state papers of the United States: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Robert Morris
Financier of the American Revolution, signed the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Elbridge Gerry
Signer of the Declaration of Independence and later became the fifth Vice President of the United States.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
Only Catholic signer of the Declaration of Independence and the longest-lived, passing away in 1832.

These individuals played significant roles in the American Revolution and the founding of the United States.
